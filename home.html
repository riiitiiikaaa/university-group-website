Why Clean Water Matters

SDG 6 is one of the most urgent global goals — here's what's at stake and what we can do.

The Global Water Crisis

Over 2.2 billion people lack access to safe drinking water. Understanding the scale and causes of the crisis is the first step toward solving it.

  • 785 million people have no basic water service
  • Women spend 200 million hours daily collecting water
  • Diarrhoea kills 530,000 children under five every year
Read More

Water Pollution

80% of the world's wastewater flows back into nature untreated. Pollution is one of the biggest threats to safe water access across the globe.

  • Industrial discharge contaminates rivers and lakes
  • 8 million tonnes of plastic enter oceans every year
  • Polluted water kills more people annually than war
Read More

Sanitation & Hygiene

3.6 billion people lack safely managed sanitation. Access to clean toilets and handwashing facilities saves millions of lives — especially children's.

  • 1 in 3 schools worldwide lacks basic toilet facilities
  • Handwashing reduces diarrhoeal disease by up to 40%
  • Poor sanitation costs countries up to 5% of their GDP
