Reflection

Feedback Page

For feedback.html, I used the W3C Markup Validation Service and found no major errors. A few minor issues were identified, including missing alt text and small HTML mistakes. I corrected these issues and confirmed that the form structure was now sound. This process reinforced the importance of clear form markup, proper field grouping, and validation checks for a reliable user experience.

Team Page

For team.html, validation passed successfully with no major errors. I used semantic HTML, including role="listitem", and applied tabindex and other accessibility features. The validation confirmed that the page structure was appropriate and accessible. This review taught me how important semantic tags and accessibility support are for creating pages that work well for all users.

Content Page

For content_ST3.html, validation also passed and the internal links, IDs, and structure were checked carefully. I fixed a few minor issues such as missing tags and nesting problems. Ensuring the page structure was clean improved navigation and helped the content remain consistent across the page. The validation step made me more attentive to code clarity and internal link reliability.

Final Reflection

In the final reflection, the validation process has shown that checks are essential in web development. Validation helps ensure accessibility, usability, and browser compatibility. It also improves attention to detail and reduces the chance of errors in the final site. This experience made it clear that validation is a valuable part of producing a professional web page.
